1What Is This Tool?

The Coupon Fab MTE Work Intake system is a web-based tool that guides your manufacturing team through the 9-phase coupon fabrication process — from the initial design request all the way through work execution and quality sign-off. It replaces disconnected spreadsheets and email chains with a single tracked workflow, and automatically syncs status to Jira.

Think of it as a digital work order that moves from desk to desk — each role sees only what they need to do, confirms their step, and passes it forward.

4Use Cases & Walkthrough Examples

Use Case 1 — Standard End-to-End

Starting a new coupon fabrication request from scratch

A stakeholder needs a new hull coupon created for weld testing. They log in, submit the request, and the system automatically routes it through all 9 phases with each role completing their part in sequence.

  • Log in as stakeholder. Click New Work Order. Fill in: Part No. SUB-CPN-0042, Description "Hull coupon — weld test specimen for rib-to-shell joint qualification", Priority High, NX Model Ref NX-Model-HullCPN-v3.2, TC Item TC-10045-B. Click Submit.
  • Log in as de_user. Open the work order → Phase 2 → confirm alignment as Design Engineer. Add comment: "NX model reviewed, design intent confirmed."
  • Log in as me_user → Phase 2 → confirm alignment as ME. Log in as weld_eng → Phase 2 → confirm as Weld Engineer.
  • Log back in as de_user. Phase 2 now shows 3/3 signed. Click Advance to Phase 3. On Phase 3, confirm NX completion, enter TC Item Reference. Click Advance.
  • Phases 4, 5, and 6 now run in parallel. Each role (Supply Chain, ME/Weld/QE, IE) completes their phase independently.
  • Log in as mechanic. Once Phases 4–7 are all done, Phase 8 unlocks. Sign off on execution. ME and QE also sign off. Advance to Phase 9.
  • Log in as qe_user. Submit the NCR assessment (no NCR for a clean close). ME and Mechanic confirm disposition. Click Close Work Order.
Result: Work order is closed. Full phase history is logged. Jira ticket reflects "Closed" status. The entire 9-phase cycle is documented with role-level sign-offs and timestamps.

Use Case 3 — Multi-Role Sign-Off (Phase 2 / Phase 5)

Three different people need to approve the same work order before it can advance

Phase 2 (alignment) and Phase 5 (work instructions) both require sign-off from three separate roles. Each person logs in independently and confirms their part — the system tracks who has signed and who hasn't.

  • After Phase 1 is submitted, have each of the three people open the app on their own device (or switch logins).
  • de_user logs in → clicks the work order → signs off as Design Engineer. Progress bar shows 1/3.
  • me_user logs in → signs off. Progress shows 2/3.
  • weld_eng logs in → signs off. Progress shows 3/3.
  • The Advance button only appears after all three sign-offs are recorded. Only the DE role sees the advance button and can push the work order forward.
Key point: Sign-offs are role-specific and persisted in the database. If someone refreshes the page, their sign-off is still there. You can't sign off twice for the same role.
Use Case 4 — Required Field Validation (Phase 1)

A team member tries to submit a form with missing information

The system won't let a work order advance if required fields are empty. Required fields are highlighted in amber when you try to submit without them.

  • Log in as stakeholder and open a new work order.
  • Leave the Part Number field blank and click Submit.
  • The Part Number field border turns amber, and an error message appears below it: "Required field."
  • Fill in the missing field and resubmit. The form advances normally.
What amber means: Any field outlined in amber is required and currently missing. Fill it in before submitting.
Use Case 5 — Checking Jira Status

Tracking where a work order is in Jira without leaving the app

Every work order is linked to a Jira MTE ticket. The dashboard shows a live Jira badge next to each work order. You can click it to open the Jira issue directly.

  • Log in to the app (any role). Open the dashboard.
  • Find your work order in the list. The blue badge next to it shows the Jira ticket number (e.g., MTE-12 ↗).
  • Click the badge — it opens the Jira issue in a new tab.
  • Each time a phase advances, the Jira ticket label is automatically updated (e.g., "phase-3-design", "phase-4-procure").
  • Phase 3 completion also transitions the Jira ticket to "In Progress" automatically.

Use Case 6 — Non-Conformance (Phase 9)

A quality issue is found during final inspection — filing an NCR

After work execution, the QE identifies a dimensional non-conformance. Phase 9 handles the NCR process with sign-offs from QE, ME, and Mechanic.

  • Log in as qe_user. Open Phase 9 for the affected work order.
  • Check the box: "Non-conformance identified — NCR required."
  • Click Submit NCR Assessment.
  • Log in as me_user → Phase 9 → Confirm Disposition as Manufacturing Engineer.
  • Log in as mechanic → Phase 9 → Confirm Disposition as Mechanic.
  • Once both dispositions are confirmed, the QE can close the work order.
If there's no issue: Leave the NCR checkbox unchecked. The system shows "No non-conformance — cleared for closure." ME and Mechanic still confirm, then the work order closes cleanly.

5Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I submitted a form but don't see the work order on the dashboard. What happened?

Check that you're logged in with the role that owns the current phase. The dashboard shows active work orders for your role. Try logging out and back in, or refresh the page.

Q: The Advance button isn't showing up in Phase 2. Why?

All three roles (DE, ME, Weld Engineer) must sign off before the Advance button appears — and only the DE login sees it. Check the sign-off progress bar. If it shows less than 3/3, at least one role hasn't confirmed yet.

Q: Can I go back to a previous phase?

You can view any completed phase using the workflow diagram (click the phase node). However, you can't re-submit or modify a phase that's already been completed and advanced. Contact the system administrator to reset a work order if needed.

Q: Why are Phases 7, 8, and 9 locked?

These phases unlock only after all upstream work is completed. Phase 8 (Work Execution) requires Phases 4, 5, 6, and 7 to all be done first — it's the convergence point. The locked screen tells you which upstream phases are still pending.

Q: Does the app require an internet connection?

The app runs fully locally. However, Jira integration does require network access to the Atlassian instance. If Jira is unavailable, work orders can still be created locally — the Jira sync will fail silently and log an error.

Q: How do I navigate between phases without a mouse?

Use the ← → arrow keys on your keyboard to move between phases in the workflow diagram. This works in both Preview and Demo modes.

Q: What does the amber highlight mean on a form field?

Amber (orange-yellow) on a field border means it's a required field that hasn't been filled in yet. You won't be able to submit the form until all amber-highlighted fields have values.
